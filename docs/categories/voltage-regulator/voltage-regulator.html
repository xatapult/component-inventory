 Category: Voltage regulators 

A voltage regulator is an electronic device or circuit that maintains a stable, constant output voltage despite changes in input voltage, load current, or other external conditions, ensuring that sensitive electronics receive clean and reliable power. It works by continuously monitoring its output and adjusting its internal resistance or switching behavior to compensate for fluctuations, preventing overvoltage or undervoltage conditions. Regulators come in linear and switching types: linear regulators provide low noise and simplicity, while switching regulators offer higher efficiency and can handle larger voltage differences. They are essential in power supplies for everything from microcontrollers to industrial equipment, where consistent voltage is critical for proper operation.
